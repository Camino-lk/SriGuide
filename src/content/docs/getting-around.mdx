Sri Lanka offers diverse transport options, from modern ride-hailing apps to iconic train journeys.
Here's how to navigate efficiently and affordably.

## Ways to move

### Use ride-hailing apps

Apps like PickMe and Uber offer fixed pricing, safety, and convenience - making them one of the best options
for getting from Bandaranaike International Airport to your hotel or next stop.

- Download the PickMe app before arrival
- Get a local SIM card at the airport for mobile data

See the [Apps](/apps) section for other essential apps to have on hand.

:::caution[Safety Note]
There is an ongoing clash between local tuk-tuk riders and ride-hailing apps, particularly PickMe, in some regions.
Exercise caution when discussing app prices or arranging pickups in public.
:::

### Regular Taxis & Tuk-Tuks

Regular taxis and tuk-tuks are widely available but may come at slightly higher prices than app-based rides.
Drivers sometimes quote inflated fares, so always agree on a price before getting in.
In hill country areas, expect higher prices due to steep terrain and increased fuel consumption.

### Public Transport

Public transport is the cheapest way to get around, though it can be inconvenient if you have heavy luggage.
And services may be infrequent in some areas. A language barrier can also be a slight challenge.

## Bargaining Tips

1. **Ask locals** what fair price is for your route
2. **Agree on price BEFORE** getting in
3. Walk away if too high—plenty more tuk-tuks
4. Use apps to avoid haggling

:::tip[A small hack]
If you use regular taxis, put your destination on either PickMe/Uber like app and know about the base price.
Then you can look for taxis and get an idea about whether the price is too high
:::

### Common Tuk-Tuk Scams

- "Meter is broken" (most don't have meters anyway)
- Detours to gem shops (politely decline)
- "Your hotel is closed" (it's not - call ahead to confirm)
- Quoting per person instead of per ride

### Iconic Train Routes

#### 1. Kandy to Ella (~7 hours)

**The Experience**: Tea plantations, mountains, misty valleys—one of the world's most scenic train rides

**Tips**:

- Book 2nd/1st class tickets in advance (online or at station)
- 3rd class is unreserved (crowded but atmospheric)
- Sit on right side Kandy→Ella for best views
- Trains can be delayed; bring snacks/water

#### 2. Colombo to Galle (~2.5 hours)

**The Experience**: Coastal route along the ocean

**Tip**: If you're traveling from colombo to Galle, sit on right side for ocean views (relative to the train's riding direction?)

### Booking Train Tickets / seats

**Online**: [https://eservices.railway.gov.lk](https://eservices.railway.gov.lk) (often crashes, book 30 days in advance)  
**At Station**:On the day of travel (3rd class always available)  
**Through Hotels**: Some guesthouses can book for small fee

**Classes**:

- **1st class**: Reserved seats, A/C or observation car (some routes)
- **2nd class**: Reserved seats, fans
- **3rd class**: Unreserved, crowded, fans/lights available but some may be faulty, authentic experience

:::caution[Peak Season]
Popular routes (Kandy-Ella) sell out quickly Dec-Feb. Book early!
:::

## Regarding buses

### Types of Buses

**Government CTB buses**: Red, older, cheapest  
**Private buses**: Varies, slightly more expensive, often faster

**Ride details**:

- Colombo to Kandy: 3-4 hours
- Colombo to Galle: 2-3 hours
- Kandy to Ella: 4-5 hours

### Tips for Bus Travel

- No reservations (first come, first served)
- Pay conductor after boarding, no automated ticket systems etc
- Crowded during rush hours
- Keep luggage with you (stay on alert if you use overhead storing)
- Google Maps shows bus routes, stops (mostly accurate)

**Best For**: Budget travelers, short distances, cultural immersion  
**Avoid If**: Carrying heavy luggage, prone to motion sickness

## Private Drivers & Vehicle Rentals

### Private Driver with Car

**Best For**:

- Groups (split cost)
- Families with kids
- Custom itineraries
- Remote areas

**How to Book**: Ask your guesthouse/hotel, or book online via tour companies

### Vehicle Rentals

Driving direction is left.

#### Cars

**Requirements**: International Driving Permit + home license

**Challenges**:

- Chaotic traffic
- Roads poorly marked
- Driving on LEFT side
- Narrow roads in hill country

**Recommended**: Only if very confident driver; most tourists prefer hiring a driver

#### Tuk-Tuks

They may provide you guidance, how to drive, instructions etc
**Requirements**: International Driving Permit, helmet (required by law)

#### Scooter/Motorcycle

**Requirements**: International Driving Permit, helmet (required by law)
**Popular Areas**: Beach towns (Mirissa, Arugam Bay, Hikkaduwa)

**Safety Notes**:

- Sri Lankan traffic is chaotic
- Roads can be poor quality
- Wear helmet ALWAYS
- Avoid night driving (poorly lit)
- Check insurance coverage

## Regional Transport Tips

### Colombo

- Use PickMe/Uber (abundant)
- Trains for coastal trips
- Avoid driving yourself (heavy traffic)

### Kandy

- Tuk-tuks or PickMe
- Central bus station for regional buses
- Train station for scenic routes to Ella

### Galle & South Coast

- Tuk-tuks or PickMe
- Buses along coast road
- Easy to explore by scooter (if experienced)

### Hill Country (Ella, Nuwara Eliya)

- Train (most scenic option)
- Private driver for flexibility
- Tuk-tuks for local trips

### East Coast (Arugam Bay, Trincomalee)

- Long bus rides or private driver
- Scooter rentals popular
- Fewer trains (east coast rail limited)

## Cost-Saving Tips

1. Use **PickMe for tuk-tuks** instead of negotiating (saves time, fair price)
2. Take **trains over buses** for long distances (more comfortable, scenic)
3. Book **3rd class train tickets** if on tight budget but be cautious about high traffic
4. Share **private drivers** with other travelers (check hostel notice boards)
5. Walk where possible—cities like Galle Fort are compact

## Safety Tips

- Don't leave bags unattended on buses/trains
- Use ride-hailing apps after dark (safer than random tuk-tuks)
- Wear helmet on scooters (police will fine you)
- Avoid driving at night (poor lighting, wildlife on roads)
- Keep small bills (LKR 100, 500, 1000) for tuk-tuks and buses

## Key phrases you may need

- **How much?**: Kiyada?
- **Too expensive**: Godak vedi
- **Please use meter**: Meter eka use karanna
- **Stop here**: Methanin navathanna
