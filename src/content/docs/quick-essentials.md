## 📋 Before You Go

### Visa & ETA (Electronic Travel Authorization)

**Good News**: Most visitors can enter Sri Lanka visa-free or with easy online ETA!

**Current Visa Policy (2025)**:
- **Free entry** for many nationalities (check current list at [eta.gov.lk](https://www.eta.gov.lk))
- **ETA required** for some countries (~USD $50-60, apply online)
- **On-arrival visa** available at airport if not pre-arranged
- **Processing**: Usually instant for online ETA

**How to Apply**:
1. Visit official site: [eta.gov.lk](https://www.eta.gov.lk)
2. Fill application (5-10 minutes)
3. Pay by credit card
4. Receive ETA via email (print or save on phone)

:::caution[Avoid Scam Sites]
Only use the official .gov.lk website. Many third-party sites charge 2-3x the actual cost.
:::

**Validity**: Typically 30 days (tourist visa)

**Verify**: Check [https://www.eta.gov.lk](https://www.eta.gov.lk) for your nationality's current requirements.

## Best Time to Visit

Sri Lanka has **two monsoon seasons** affecting different regions:

### Southwest (Colombo, Galle, Hill Country)
- **Best**: December - March (dry, peak season)
- **Monsoon**: May - September (heavy rain, southwest coast)

### Northeast (Trincomalee, Arugam Bay)
- **Best**: April - September
- **Monsoon**: October - January

### Year-Round Options
- **Ella, Kandy** (Hill Country): Cooler, can visit anytime (expect rain May-Sept)
- **Colombo**: Manageable year-round

**Peak Season**: December - February (best weather, most crowded, higher prices)  
**Budget Season**: May - September (rain but fewer tourists, cheaper)

## Currency & Money

### Sri Lankan Rupee (LKR)

**Exchange Rate** (Dec 2025): ~LKR 300-330 = 1 USD (check current rates)

**Where to Exchange**:
- **Airport**: Convenient but slightly lower rates
- **Banks**: Best rates, longer queues
- **Licensed money changers**: Good rates in cities
- **Avoid**: Unlicensed street changers

### ATMs

- Widely available in cities and tourist areas
- Accept Visa, Mastercard, Maestro
- Withdrawal limits: ~LKR 50,000-100,000 per transaction
- Fees: ~LKR 500-1,000 + your bank's international fee
- **Best ATMs**: Commercial Bank, Sampath Bank (reliable)

:::tip[Cash is King]
Many small shops, tuk-tuks, and guesthouses only take cash. Always keep LKR 5,000-10,000 for daily needs.
:::

### Credit Cards

- Accepted at hotels, restaurants, and larger shops
- Less common at small guesthouses and local eateries
- Inform your bank before traveling to avoid fraud blocks

## Daily Budget Estimates

### Budget Traveler
- **Accommodation**: LKR 2,000-4,000 ($7-15) - guesthouses, hostels
- **Food**: LKR 1,500-2,500 ($5-8) - local restaurants, street food
- **Transport**: LKR 1,000-2,000 ($3-7) - buses, trains, short tuk-tuks
- **Total**: ~$20-30 per day

### Mid-Range Traveler
- **Accommodation**: LKR 8,000-15,000 ($25-50) - nice guesthouses, small hotels
- **Food**: LKR 3,000-5,000 ($10-15) - mix of local and tourist restaurants
- **Transport**: LKR 2,000-5,000 ($7-15) - private drivers, PickMe rides
- **Total**: ~$50-80 per day

### Comfort Traveler
- **Accommodation**: LKR 20,000+ ($60+) - boutique hotels, resorts
- **Food**: LKR 5,000-10,000 ($15-30) - hotels, upscale dining
- **Transport**: LKR 10,000+ ($30+) - private car with driver
- **Total**: ~$120+ per day

**Add**: Entry fees to attractions ($5-30), activities (safaris $30-80), alcohol (expensive due to taxes)

## SIM Cards & Internet

### Getting a SIM at Airport

**Best Option**: Buy at **Bandaranaike International Airport** (Colombo)

**Providers**:
- **Dialog** (most reliable, best coverage)
- **Mobitel** (good coverage)
- **Hutch** (budget option)

**Where**: Look for booths after customs, before exit  
**Cost**: ~LKR 500-1,500 ($2-5) for SIM + initial data  
**Data Packages**: ~LKR 500-2,000 for 10-50GB (valid 7-30 days)  
**What to Bring**: Passport (required for registration)

### Tourist SIM Plans (Dialog)

- **7-day**: ~50GB data + calls for ~LKR 1,500
- **14-day**: ~100GB data for ~LKR 2,500
- **30-day**: ~150GB data for ~LKR 3,500

**Coverage**: Good in cities and tourist areas; spotty in remote mountains

### WiFi Availability

- Most hotels and guesthouses have free WiFi
- Cafes and restaurants in tourist areas offer WiFi
- Quality varies (often slow)
- Consider mobile data for reliability

## Packing Essentials

### Must-Bring Items

- **Mosquito repellent** (DEET-based, 30%+) - crucial!
- **Sunscreen** (SPF 50+) - strong tropical sun
- **Reusable water bottle** (with filter if possible)
- **Light, modest clothing** (shoulders/knees covered for temples)
- **Comfortable walking shoes** and sandals
- **Rain jacket** or small umbrella (especially monsoon season)
- **Power adapter** (Type D/G/M plugs, 230V)
- **Photocopy of passport** + emergency contacts

### Nice-to-Have

- Flashlight/headlamp (power cuts can happen)
- Small backpack for day trips
- Sarong (multi-use: beach, temple cover-up, towel)
- Basic first-aid kit
- Tissues/toilet paper (not always available)
- Ziploc bags (protect electronics from humidity)
- Book or Kindle (for long train rides)

### What NOT to Pack

- Heavy clothes (it's HOT year-round except hill country)
- Too much cash (ATMs everywhere)
- Expensive jewelry (leave valuables at home)

## Clothing Tips

### Temple-Appropriate Attire

- **Cover shoulders** (no tank tops)
- **Cover knees** (long pants, skirts, dresses)
- **Remove shoes** before entering temples
- Keep modest clothing in daypack for impromptu temple visits

### Beach vs. Cultural Sites

- **Beaches**: Swimwear okay, but avoid walking through towns in bikini/swim trunks
- **Cities**: Dress modestly, especially for women
- **Hill country**: Bring light jacket or sweater (cooler temps)

## Time Zone

Sri Lanka Standard Time: **UTC +5:30**

No daylight saving time.

## Language

- **Official**: Sinhala, Tamil
- **Widely spoken**: English (especially in tourist areas, hotels, restaurants)
- **Tourist areas**: Most tuk-tuk drivers, shop owners speak basic English
- **Rural areas**: English less common—learning a few Sinhala phrases helps!

### Useful Phrases

- Hello: **Ayubowan** (ah-yu-bo-wan)
- Thank you: **Bohoma sthuthi** (bo-ho-ma stoo-tee)
- How much?: **Kiyada?** (kee-ya-da)
- Not too spicy: **Vadi katas venna epa** (va-dee ka-tas wen-na eh-pa)
- Delicious: **Rasa** (ra-sa)

## Power & Electricity

- **Voltage**: 230V, 50Hz
- **Plugs**: Type D, G, M (round pins)
- **Adapter needed**: Yes, for most international devices
- **Power cuts**: Occasional, especially during monsoon

## Arrival at Airport

**Bandaranaike International Airport (CMB)** - Main airport near Colombo

### Steps After Landing:

1. **Visa/ETA check** (have ETA printout or on phone)
2. **Collect luggage**
3. **Customs** (declare if carrying over $15,000 or significant goods)
4. **SIM card** (get here if needed)
5. **Currency exchange** or **ATM withdrawal**
6. **Transport** to your destination

### Airport to Colombo/Negombo:

- **PickMe/Uber**: ~LKR 1,500-2,000 to Negombo (15-20 min), ~LKR 2,500-3,500 to Colombo (45 min)
- **Prepaid taxi**: Available at counter, slightly more expensive
- **Bus**: Cheapest (~LKR 150-250) but not convenient with luggage

See our [Getting Around](/en/getting-around/) guide for detailed transport info.

## Quick Safety Tips

- Save **1912** (Tourist Police) in your phone
- Don't drink tap water
- Agree on tuk-tuk price BEFORE getting in
- Modest dress for temples
- Keep small bills for daily transactions
