## General Safety Tips

- Share your itinerary with someone back home
- Keep copies of passport and insurance in cloud storage
- Have emergency contacts saved offline
- Keep small amount of local cash for emergencies
- Know your accommodation's address in local language
- Heavy rains can cause flash floods and landslides (May-Sept southwest, Oct-Jan northeast)
- Keep distance from wild elephants and monkeys. Call forest rangers if threatened.
- If you feel an earthquake (very rare in Sri Lanka) at the coast, move inland immediately.

### What to Do in Emergencies

Stay calm and call for help. Assess the situation and decide whether you should call any authority based on the situation.
Note that, you can dial `119` emergency number (from any mobile device, even without a SIM card) to contact police for an emergency situation.

**Phrases you may need:**

- Help (me) - උදව් කරන්න `udav karanna` <Speak src="/audio/Help-me.mp3" title="Help (me)"/>
- I'm lost - මම අතරමං වෙලා `mama ataramaṁ velā` <Speak src="/audio/I-am-lost.mp3" title="I'm lost"/>

### Natural Disasters (Floods, Landslides)

- Follow local weather alerts/warnings issued by [Department of Meteorology](https://www.meteo.gov.lk/), [Disaster Management Center](https://www.dmc.gov.lk/) & local media.
- In case of a flood/tsunami warning, move to higher grounds immediately.
- If you are in danger, call `117`/`119` emergency services.
- If necessary, contact Sri Lanka Tourism Hotline - `1912` and alert your embassy/consulate.

### Theft or Crime

- Call for help, let others know that you are in danger.
- Get to a safe, public place if needed and call police via `119`.
- Record details (time, place, descriptions) and file a police report.
- Contact your bank and block/cancel your stolen bank cards.

:::note[Lost Passport]
Contact your embassy immediately if you lose your passport. Most embassies require a police report before issuing emergency travel documents.
:::

### Lost in Remote Areas

- Check map/phone, note landmarks and last-known route.
- Try reaching emergency services by dialling the above numbers if needed.
- Seek help from locals, use Sinhala phrases from [here](/quick-essentials#useful-phrases), if needed to communicate with people.
